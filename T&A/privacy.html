Privacy Policy

Last updated: January 2025

ExpenseFlow is a personal finance tracking application. It is a non-commercial personal project and is provided “as is.” This Privacy Policy explains how data is collected, processed, and stored in accordance with the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR).

1. Data Controller

For GDPR purposes, the data controller is the individual developer who built ExpenseFlow.
If you require clarification or deletion of your data, you may contact the developer using the GitHub profile associated with this project.

2. What Data Is Collected

2.1. If you sign in with Google

When choosing Google Sign-In, the following information is processed:

  • Your Google User ID (UID) – obtained from the Google Identity token ("sub" field)
  • Your financial data entered into the application (expenses, categories, notes, utilities, car expenses, etc.)

No email address, profile picture, name, or additional personal data is collected.

2.2. Visitor Mode

In visitor mode, no personal data is sent to any server. All information is stored locally in your browser using localStorage and is deleted when your browser data is cleared.

3. Purpose of Data Processing

Data is processed exclusively for:

  • Allowing you to log in and access your individual financial entries
  • Saving your expense data so it is accessible across sessions
  • Providing app functionality (charts, summaries, monthly reports)

No profiling, marketing, or automated decision-making takes place.

4. Legal Basis for Processing (GDPR Article 6)

  • Art. 6(1)(b) – Processing is necessary for providing the service (saving and retrieving your personal finance data)
  • Art. 6(1)(a) – Your consent when you choose to sign in with Google

5. Where and How Data Is Stored

If you sign in with Google, data is stored in Firebase Firestore (Google Cloud), under your unique Google UID. Storage is handled by Google’s secure infrastructure.

In visitor mode, data is stored only locally in your browser.

6. Cookies

ExpenseFlow does not set any cookies of its own. However, Google Identity Services may set authentication cookies necessary for:

  • Login and session management
  • Security and fraud prevention

No analytics, tracking, or advertising cookies are used by ExpenseFlow.

7. Your GDPR Rights

You have the following rights:

  • Right of access – You may request details of your stored data
  • Right to rectification – You may correct inaccurate data
  • Right to erasure (“right to be forgotten”) – You may request data deletion
  • Right to restriction of processing
  • Right to data portability (export your data)
  • Right to withdraw consent – by deleting your Firebase account data

Firebase does not allow direct remote deletion by users, so you may contact the developer to request data removal.

8. Data Retention

  • Data stored in Firebase is kept until you request deletion.
  • Data in visitor mode exists only locally and is deleted by your browser.

9. Third-Party Services

The application uses:

  • Google Identity Services – for authentication
  • Firebase / Google Cloud – for secure data storage

No other third-party services are used.

10. Contact

For data deletion or inquiries, you may contact the developer via the GitHub profile connected to this project.
